Introduction to Swami Vivekananda Scholarship (SVMCM)

The Swami Vivekananda Merit-cum-Means Scholarship (SVMCM) is one of the most impactful financial aid programs launched by the Government of West Bengal. It aims to assist economically weaker yet meritorious students pursuing higher studies within the state. The scholarship encourages young minds to continue education without financial stress, promoting academic excellence at every level — from higher secondary to postgraduate and professional courses.

Thousands of students apply for this scheme every year through the official Swami Vivekananda Scholarship portal. However, many applicants remain uncertain about whether they meet the eligibility criteria. This detailed 2025 guide explains every academic, income, and institutional requirement for SVMCM so that students can apply with confidence.

General Eligibility Conditions

The Swami Vivekananda Scholarship program follows a set of common eligibility rules applicable to all categories. Every applicant must meet these baseline conditions before applying under any specific educational level:

  • ✅ Applicant must be a permanent resident of West Bengal.
  • ✅ The student must be studying in an institution located in West Bengal.
  • ✅ The family’s annual income must not exceed ₹2,50,000 from all sources.
  • ✅ The student should have good academic standing in the qualifying examination.
  • ✅ Applicants must not be receiving any other central or state-level scholarship during the same academic session.

Eligibility Criteria by Academic Level

The scholarship covers multiple stages of education — from higher secondary students to those enrolled in medical, engineering, and postgraduate programs. Below is a level-wise breakdown of eligibility requirements for each category.

1. Higher Secondary (HS) Level

Students who have passed the Madhyamik (Class 10) examination and are currently studying in Class 11 or 12 in any recognized West Bengal school or institution can apply under this category.

  • Minimum required marks in Madhyamik: 75% aggregate.
  • Applicant must be studying under the West Bengal Council of Higher Secondary Education (WBCHSE).
  • Course must be regular (not open or distance mode).

2. Undergraduate (UG) Level

Students enrolling in undergraduate courses in Arts, Science, or Commerce streams can apply after completing Class 12.

  • Minimum marks in Class 12 (WBCHSE/ISC/CBSE or equivalent): 75% aggregate.
  • Course must be full-time and offered by a recognized college or university in West Bengal.
  • Professional degree courses such as B.Tech, MBBS, or Nursing have separate categories (see below).

3. Postgraduate (PG) Level

Postgraduate students pursuing MA, MSc, or MCom programs from recognized West Bengal universities can also benefit from SVMCM.

  • Minimum marks in Honours (UG) subject: 53% for general courses.
  • For professional courses like Engineering or Technology, minimum 55% marks are required at graduation level.

4. Diploma and Polytechnic Students

Students enrolled in Polytechnic or Diploma programs after Madhyamik or HS are also eligible if studying in government or government-recognized institutions in West Bengal.

  • Minimum qualifying marks: 75% in the last board examination.
  • Course must be approved by the State Council for Technical Education (WBSCTE).

5. Engineering and Professional Courses

The SVMCM also covers students pursuing technical or professional degrees such as B.Tech, MBBS, BDS, Nursing, Pharmacy, or Law.

  • Minimum marks in HS or equivalent: 75%.
  • Course must be recognized by AICTE, MCI, or respective regulatory bodies.
  • Institution must be located within West Bengal.

Income Criteria Explained

One of the key conditions under SVMCM is the family income cap of ₹2,50,000 per annum. This limit ensures that the benefit reaches students from economically weaker backgrounds. Applicants must upload a valid Income Certificate issued by one of the following:

  • District Magistrate or Additional District Magistrate
  • Sub-Divisional Officer (SDO)
  • Block Development Officer (BDO)
  • Revenue Officer (not below the rank of SI)

Certificates older than six months are not accepted. Self-declared income affidavits without official signatures are also invalid.

Institutional and Course Requirements

Students must be enrolled in recognized educational institutions within the geographical limits of West Bengal. Courses must be full-time and affiliated to approved boards or universities. Distance or correspondence courses are not eligible under SVMCM, as the scholarship prioritizes regular attendance and academic engagement.

Private institutions offering professional courses must have valid AICTE, NCTE, or MCI approval. For universities, both state and central universities functioning in West Bengal are eligible.

Special Categories and Renewal Eligibility

Students under minority or backward categories (SC, ST, OBC-A, OBC-B) can also apply under SVMCM for Minorities via the Aikyashree portal. However, they must choose only one scholarship scheme to avoid duplication.

For renewal, students must secure at least 60% marks in the previous exam and continue in the same course level without any break. They should apply for renewal every academic year before the closing date.

Documents Required for Application

  • Recent passport-size photograph
  • Marksheet of the last qualifying exam
  • Admission receipt or ongoing course certificate
  • Income certificate issued by competent authority
  • Bank account details (in applicant’s name)
  • Aadhaar card and domicile proof

Conclusion

The Swami Vivekananda Scholarship 2025 stands as a lifeline for West Bengal’s students striving to achieve higher education against economic challenges. Understanding the eligibility criteria is the first step toward a successful application. Whether you are in higher secondary, pursuing graduation, or enrolled in a professional degree, ensure your marks, income proof, and institutional documents meet the prescribed standards.

By fulfilling these criteria and applying on time, you can secure vital financial support that fuels your educational journey and contributes to the vision of Swami Vivekananda — empowering youth through knowledge and discipline.
